üí∞ Prepare Accurate Cost Estimates
‚ñº

Step-by-Step Guide

  1. Obtain Project Documentation
    Review tender documents, architectural drawings, and electrical schematics. Identify system size (kW), roof type, mounting requirements, and electrical infrastructure.
  2. Conduct Site Assessment Review
    Analyze site photos, shading analysis, roof orientation, and structural capacity reports. Note any access restrictions or special equipment needs.
  3. Calculate System Components
    Determine panel quantity, inverter sizing, mounting hardware, cabling requirements, and switchboard upgrades needed.
  4. Price All Materials
    Get current pricing from approved suppliers for panels, inverters, racking, cables, and electrical components. Factor in bulk discounts and delivery costs.
  5. Estimate Labour Hours
    Calculate installation time based on system size, roof complexity, and crew size. Include design, installation, electrical, and commissioning hours.
  6. Add Project Costs
    Include permits, engineering certifications, scaffolding, crane hire, insurance, and project management overhead.
  7. Apply Margins & Contingencies
    Add profit margin (typically 15-25%) and contingency (5-10%) for unforeseen issues or price fluctuations.
  8. Compile Final Estimate
    Create detailed breakdown showing materials, labour, project costs, margins, and total project value with GST.
üí° Pro Tip: Always maintain a database of historical project costs to benchmark new estimates. Track actual vs estimated costs to improve accuracy over time.
üìä Example: 100kW Commercial Rooftop Materials: $65,000 | Labour: $18,000 | Project Costs: $7,000 | Subtotal: $90,000 | Margin (20%): $18,000 | Contingency (7%): $7,560 | Total (ex GST): $115,560
üìä Develop BOMs, Labour Models & Margins
‚ñº

BOM Development Process

  1. Create Master Component List
    List all materials: solar panels, inverters, mounting rails, clamps, cables (DC & AC), connectors, isolators, switchgear, conduit, and labelling.
  2. Calculate Quantities
    Use system design to determine exact quantities. Add 5% wastage for cables and consumables. Include installation hardware and fixings.
  3. Source Current Pricing
    Obtain quotes from minimum 3 suppliers. Negotiate volume discounts. Verify stock availability and lead times.
  4. Build Labour Model
    Break down by task: site prep (4hrs), panel installation (0.5hr/panel), electrical (8hrs/100kW), testing & commissioning (4hrs). Multiply by hourly rate ($65-$85/hr).
  5. Calculate Total Costs
    Sum all material costs + labour costs. Add equipment hire (crane, scissor lift, scaffolding) if required.
  6. Apply Margin Strategy
    Standard projects: 15-20% margin. Complex/risk: 25-30% margin. High-volume/competitive: 10-15% margin.
  7. Set Contingency Levels
    Standard projects: 5-7%. Older buildings: 8-12%. First-time customer/complex site: 10-15%.
  8. Document Assumptions
    Note all assumptions: crane access, roof condition, electrical capacity, weather allowances, and any exclusions.
üí° Pro Tip: Create template BOMs for common system sizes (50kW, 100kW, 200kW) to speed up estimation. Update templates quarterly with current pricing.

üìê Review Drawings, Specs & Scopes
‚ñº

Document Review Checklist

  1. Review Architectural Drawings
    Check roof plans, elevations, and structural details. Identify roof type (metal, concrete, tile), pitch, orientation, and dimensions. Note skylights, vents, and obstructions.
  2. Analyze Electrical Drawings
    Review single-line diagrams, switchboard layouts, and cable routes. Verify existing capacity, available space for new equipment, and connection points.
  3. Study Technical Specifications
    Read equipment requirements (panel/inverter specs), performance criteria, warranty requirements, and Australian Standards compliance (AS/NZS 5033, AS/NZS 3000).
  4. Understand Project Scope
    Identify what's included: design, supply, installation, commissioning, documentation. Note exclusions: upgrades to existing infrastructure, structural modifications.
  5. Check for Conflicts
    Look for inconsistencies between drawings and specifications. Identify clashes with HVAC, fire systems, or other services.
  6. Assess Complexity Factors
    Evaluate access challenges, working at heights requirements, heritage overlays, council restrictions, and grid connection complexity.
  7. Prepare RFI List
    Document all unclear items, missing information, or conflicts. Submit Request for Information (RFI) to client or consultant promptly.
  8. Document Assumptions
    Record all assumptions made where information is unclear. These protect your estimate and tender submission.
‚ö†Ô∏è Common Pitfalls: Don't assume roof structural capacity is adequate. Always flag if structural certification is not provided. Missing this can lead to major cost blowouts.
üí° Pro Tip: Create a standard RFI template for missing information. Address: structural certification, electrical capacity, access restrictions, roof warranty implications, and asbestos reports.

üíµ Obtain & Validate Pricing
‚ñº

Pricing Procurement Process

  1. Identify Suppliers & Subcontractors
    Maintain database of approved suppliers for panels, inverters, mounting, electrical. Vet subcontractors for licensing, insurance, and track record.
  2. Prepare RFQ Package
    Create clear scope of work, specifications, quantities, delivery requirements, and payment terms. Include project timeline and site location.
  3. Request Multiple Quotes
    Send RFQ to minimum 3 suppliers per category. Ensure like-for-like comparisons with identical specifications. Set clear response deadline.
  4. Evaluate Quotations
    Compare pricing, delivery times, warranty terms, and payment conditions. Check for hidden costs: freight, after-hours delivery, crane unloading.
  5. Validate Pricing Accuracy
    Cross-reference against recent projects and market benchmarks. Query any prices that seem unusually high or low. Verify stock availability for quoted items.
  6. Check Supplier Credentials
    Confirm current insurance (public liability $20M+, product liability). Verify manufacturer authorizations and warranty support. Check references for reliability.
  7. Negotiate Terms
    Negotiate volume discounts for multi-project commitments. Discuss payment terms (typically 30-day account). Clarify defect warranty and return policies.
  8. Document & File
    Save all quotes with date stamps and validity periods. Create comparison spreadsheet. Update pricing database for future estimates.
‚ö†Ô∏è Red Flags: Prices >20% below market average often indicate inferior quality or grey imports. Verify product certifications (CEC approved) and Australian warranty support before acceptance.
üí° Pro Tip: Build long-term relationships with 2-3 preferred suppliers per category. Better pricing, priority stock allocation, and extended payment terms come with proven track record and volume commitment.
üìÑ Support Tenders, RFQs & Proposals
‚ñº

Tender Preparation Guide

  1. Understand Tender Requirements
    Read tender documents thoroughly. Create compliance checklist for all mandatory requirements. Note evaluation criteria and weightings (price, quality, experience, program).
  2. Develop Pricing Strategy
    Assess competition level and project desirability. Decide margin based on risk, opportunity value, and relationship potential. Balance competitiveness with profitability.
  3. Create Detailed Breakdown
    Provide transparent cost breakdown: materials (by category), labour (by task), project costs, margins. Show clear methodology and assumptions.
  4. Write Technical Methodology
    Describe installation approach, safety procedures, quality control, and program. Address specific site challenges and proposed solutions. Include staging and sequencing.
  5. Compile Company Credentials
    Include relevant project experience (similar size/type), client references, team qualifications (CEC accredited designers/installers), certifications (AS/NZS 5033, electrical license).
  6. Develop Program Schedule
    Create realistic timeline from order to commissioning. Show key milestones: design, procurement, installation, testing, handover. Allow for weather delays and approvals.
  7. Address Evaluation Criteria
    Tailor response to match evaluation criteria. Highlight strengths in price, quality, experience, safety, and innovation. Provide evidence for claims.
  8. Quality Check & Submit
    Review for completeness, accuracy, and clarity. Check all calculations. Ensure professional presentation. Submit before deadline with confirmation of receipt.
üí° Pro Tip: Create template sections for common requirements: company overview, safety policy, quality procedures, standard T&Cs. Customize for each tender but maintain core content library.

Key Terms & Concepts

kW vs kWh:

‚Ä¢ kW (Kilowatt) = System power capacity (size)
‚Ä¢ kWh (Kilowatt-hour) = Energy produced/consumed
‚Ä¢ Example: 100kW system produces ~150,000 kWh/year

Solar Panel Types:

‚Ä¢ Monocrystalline - Most efficient (20-22%), black, premium
‚Ä¢ Polycrystalline - Good efficiency (15-17%), blue, cost-effective
‚Ä¢ Bifacial - Captures light both sides, 10-20% extra production

Inverter Types:

‚Ä¢ String Inverters - Most common, cost-effective
‚Ä¢ Microinverters - One per panel, best for shading
‚Ä¢ Hybrid - Includes battery integration capability

Key Performance Metrics:

‚Ä¢ Peak Sun Hours - Melbourne: 4.5hrs/day average
‚Ä¢ System Efficiency - Typically 75-85% (losses from heat, inverter, cables)
‚Ä¢ Capacity Factor - Actual vs potential production (15-25%)
‚Ä¢ Degradation - Panels lose 0.5-0.7% efficiency per year

Australian Standards:

‚Ä¢ AS/NZS 5033 - Installation standard for PV arrays
‚Ä¢ AS/NZS 3000 - Electrical wiring rules
‚Ä¢ CEC Approval - Clean Energy Council product approval
‚Ä¢ STCs - Small-scale Technology Certificates (rebate)

2. Number of Panels:

3. Annual Production:
Annual kWh = System Size (kW) √ó Peak Sun Hours √ó 365 √ó Efficiency
Example: 100 √ó 4.5 √ó 365 √ó 0.80 = 131,400 kWh/year
4. Project Cost:
Total Cost = (System Size (W) √ó Cost per Watt) √ó (1 + Margin%)
Example: (100,000 √ó $1.10) √ó 1.20 = $132,000
5. Payback Period:
Years = Total Cost √∑ (Annual Production √ó Electricity Rate)
